Bill of Rights

The first ten amendments comprise the Bill of Rights

First Amendment

"Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the Government for a redress of grievances."

What this Means:

This amendment protects freedom of speech, religion, the press, the right to protest peacefully, and to petition the government. It ensures people can express themselves and their ideas without fear of punishment from the government.

Second Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment gives people the right to own and carry weapons. It was originally connected to the idea of state militias, but today it’s mostly used to support private gun ownership.

Third Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

The government cannot force people to house soldiers in their homes during peacetime. It was created in response to British practices before the Revolutionary War.

Fourth Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment protects people from being searched or having their things taken by the government without a good reason. Police usually need a warrant, approved by a judge, before searching someone’s home or belongings.

Fifth Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment gives people the right to remain silent and not testify against themselves in court. It also protects against double jeopardy (being tried twice for the same crime) and says the government must follow fair legal steps before taking your property or freedom.

Sixth Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment guarantees a fair trial if someone is accused of a crime. It includes the right to a lawyer, to know what you’re being accused of, and to have a jury of peers.

Seventh Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment says people have the right to a jury trial in civil cases, which are cases between people or companies about money or property. It helps make sure regular citizens, not just judges, have a say in legal decisions.

Eighth Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment protects people from being given very high fines or punishments that are too harsh or unfair. It makes sure punishments fit the crime.

Ninth Amendment

"A well-regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed"

What this means:

This amendment gives people the right to own and carry weapons. It was originally connected to the idea of state militias, but today it’s mostly used to support private gun ownership.
